Integrated Vehicle-Living Concepts for Lunar Exploration

These concepts explore innovative approaches to integrating vehicle mobility and living quarters for sustained lunar exploration missions.

  • Integrated Rover-Habitat Concepts: Designs that combine transportation and living space into a single unit, maximizing mobility and efficiency.
  • Modular Mobile Habitats: Mobile units with interchangeable modules for various functions such as living quarters, labs, and storage.
  • Expandable Rover-Habitats: Structures that start compact during transport but expand to provide larger living and working spaces on the lunar surface.
  • Multi-Functional Vehicles: Rovers with adaptable interiors that can serve different purposes like sleeping quarters, labs, or workshops.
  • Resource Utilization: Integration of in-situ resource utilization (ISRU) to extract and process lunar resources for energy and habitat materials.
  • Autonomous and Teleoperated Systems: Vehicles equipped with advanced navigation and remote operation capabilities for safe lunar surface mobility.
  • Radiation Protection and Life Support: Robust systems for radiation shielding and life support to ensure astronaut safety and habitability.
  • International Collaboration: Cooperation among space agencies and private entities to develop and test these concepts for lunar exploration.
