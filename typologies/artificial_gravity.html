Artificial Gravity on the Moon

Artificial gravity can be achieved on the moon using centrifugal force by rotating at different speeds depending on the radius of rotation. Below are the details for achieving artificial gravity equal to that on Earth at different radii:

Summary
Radius (meters) RPM Speed (km/h) Angle relative to Moon Surface (degrees)
5 6.11 11.6 63.4
15 3.83 21.7 36.4
35 2.61 34.4 29.1

These values represent the rotational speed in revolutions per minute (RPM), the corresponding speed in kilometers per hour (km/h), and the angle relative to the moon's surface required to simulate gravity equal to that on Earth at each specified radius on the moon.
